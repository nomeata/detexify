What is this?

There are plenty of interesting symbols in the unicode specification to beef up chats or what else. But they have to be found first, and the keyboard is just too small. There are lists, of course, but they are hard to search. Deunicodeify is an attempt to simplify this search.

How does it work?

Just draw the symbol you are looking for into the square area above and look what happens!

My symbol isn't found!

The symbol may not be trained enough or it is not yet in the list of supported symbols. In the first case you can do the training yourself. In the second case just drop me a line (mail@joachim-breitner.de)!

I like this. How can I help?

You could spare some time training Deunicodeify. You could also look at the source on GitHub and if you want to contribute you're welcome. If you really, really like this you can also donate to the original author.

This doesn't work at all!?

Deunicodeify relies on HTML5 features so you need a fairly recent browser. Opera 9.6 / Firefox 3.5 / Safari 4 should do the trick.

Who created Deunicodeify?

Philipp Kühl had the initial idea and Daniel Kirsch created Detexify, where you can classify LaTeX characters. Joachim Breitner modified the interface to search unicode characters, but this is just a minor modification of Daniel’s Detexify, so all the praise belongs to him.

Did this help? Hosting Detexify costs money and if it helps you may consider helping to pay the hosting bill.
